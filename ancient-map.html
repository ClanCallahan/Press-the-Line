⚜ Your Empire ⚜

The Ancient World

The map spans from Hellas to Persia. Blue cities are Greek, red are Persian. Yellow dashed lines mark the Persian Royal Road — armies travel it faster. Striped lines are supply routes linking friendly cities. Together, these routes form supply networks.

Time & Treasury

Press Start Time — each full orbit around the sun is one year. Cities with a population of 20,000 or greater produce arms with a Military Blacksmith . All cities grow population and produce gold, and pay your armies' yearly salaries. The Empire scroll controls tax rate: high taxes fund wars but slow population growth.

Raising Armies

Tap one of your cities → Raise an Army. Troops (except Generals) cost population from the city, advanced arms from your stockpile, and gold from the treasury. Each class (Melee, Archers, Cavalry) comes in variants — some unlocked via temple favor.

Campaigning

Tap an army to Move, Merge, or Attack. Gold rings mark eligible targets. Armies that are larger than 10% of the population of the supply network where they are located show a red ring and lose troops rapidly to starvation. Well-supplied armies will show a green ring. Protect your supply lines to keep your armies alive.

Battle & Conquest

Attacks load the battlefield where you can control troops by hand. Win it and choose the city's fate: Capture (keep it as-is), Enslave (scatter its people across your empire — mostly empties this city but fills your other cities), or Erase (put it to the sword and colonize with your own — reestablish the city and rewrite its history).

The Gods

Each side worships six gods. Build a temple to a god in one of your cities to unlock the next tech in its domain — better armor, damage, movement, or rare units like the Alexandrian phalanx or war elephants. Temples take three years to raise. Visit the Gods temple on the bottom-right to begin.

⚒ Lose a Military Blacksmith?

Recruiting this army will reduce the city population below 20,000, causing you to lose a Military Blacksmith that produces advanced arms. Are you sure you want to create this army?

⚠ Insufficient Food

CAUTION — Insufficient food! Your troops will starve — the raised troops would exceed 10% of the total population on this supply network, resulting in immediate attrition.
